Radical Women: Latin American Art, 1960–1985
Starting on September 15th and running for fifteen weeks, The Hammer Museum is proud and excited to present our newest exhibition. Part of Pacific Standard Time: LA/LA, this exhibition reappraises the contribution of Latin American women artists and those of Latino and Chicano heritage in the United States to contemporary art.

Radical Women Walk-through:
A Live Tour With Elena Shtromberg


In this unique gallery talk, Elena Shtromberg, associate professor in the Department of Art and Art History at the University of Utah, discusses specific works from Radical Women that inspire and provoke her.

Talleres: Experimental Women Filmmakers from Latin America

 THURSDAY NOV 30, 2017 7:30PM
Showcasing rare works by female filmmakers who carved out a place within the male-dominated world of Latin American independent film, this program includes Uruguayan filmmaker Lydia García Millán’s Color (1955); Narcisa Hirsch’s Workshop (1975); and recent video essays by Mexican artist Ximena Cuevas. Curated by Ángela López Ruiz.
